Mission, Ethos & Faith

Waypoint Institute exists to obey the Great Commission with beauty, truth, and accessibility. Our cohorts pursue Biblical Formation together, rooted in Christ and welcoming disciples from every nation.

“Go therefore and make disciples of all nations… teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you.”

Matthew 28:19–20

Our Mission

Waypoint Institute forms resilient disciples who know Scripture, love the church, and live as witnesses wherever God places them. We do this through tuition-free cohorts, pastoral presence, and capstone conversations that call each student to faithful obedience.

We steward donor generosity to offer rigorous theological learning without barriers so believers in restricted or resource-limited contexts can be trained for gospel ministry.

How Mission Shapes the Year

  • 24-person cohorts stay together through the Biblical Formation core.
  • Courses run on a shared calendar with self-paced weeks and live conversations.
  • Capstone dialogues function as oral examinations to affirm mastery and shepherd growth.
  • Associate pathways reunite the cohort for collaborative research and witness.

Our Ethos

These commitments flow from Christ and guide every lesson, discussion, and decision.

Christocentrism

Jesus is the center of history, the lens for Scripture, and the anchor of our hope. Every course, conversation, and assessment aims to magnify His lordship.

Faithful Stewardship of Truth

We seek “whatever is true, honorable, just, pure…” (Phil. 4:8). Scripture remains our supreme authority, and we study the wider world so that every useful insight is submitted to Christ for the good of His church.

Radical Accessibility

Where others add burdens, we clear paths. Cohorts adapt to local realities, use secure tools, and provide language support so believers who were once excluded can participate fully.

Mere-Christian Statement of Faith

We gladly stand within the historic, orthodox confession shared by believers across traditions. These points summarize what our faculty, leaders, and students affirm.

  • Triune God: One God in three persons—Father, Son, and Holy Spirit—worthy of all worship.
  • Jesus Christ: The eternal Son made flesh, fully God and fully man, crucified, risen, and returning in glory.
  • Salvation by grace: Humanity is separated from God by sin and reconciled only through Christ’s atoning work, received by grace through faith.
  • Scripture: The Old and New Testaments are the inspired, trustworthy, and authoritative Word of God.
  • The Holy Spirit: The Spirit indwells believers, empowering holy living, witness, and the unity of the church.
  • The Church: One holy, catholic, and apostolic people called to worship, disciple, and serve.
  • The Sacraments: Baptism and the Lord’s Supper are ordained by Christ as signs and seals of the gospel.
  • Hope of resurrection: Christ will judge the living and the dead, raising His people to everlasting life in the new creation.
