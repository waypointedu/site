How Waypoint Works

A hybrid delivery model that scales serious learning while prioritizing safety in restricted contexts.

Course Delivery Timeline

1

First Runs: Live Sessions

Faculty teach 90-minute live sessions in English over 5–10 weeks. Lecture portions are recorded for open cohorts only. Students participate in discussion, complete readings, and submit assignments (if pursuing a certificate).

  • Live Zoom sessions with faculty
  • Real-time Q&A and discussion
  • Recordings for open cohorts (lecture only, not discussion)
  • Funded by donors (~$500 per course)
2

Re-Runs: Cohorts with Table Leaders

After the first run, recordings (where available) are paired with trained table leaders who guide 12–15 students through weekly discussions. Leaders review assignments, provide pastoral feedback, and create accountable learning communities.

  • Watch recorded lectures between sessions (open cohorts)
  • Meet weekly with table leader for 90-minute discussion
  • Complete readings and assignments on the same schedule
  • Pass/No-Record grading for certificate track
3

Secure Cohorts: Audio-Only, Pseudonyms

For learners in restricted contexts (e.g., Afghanistan, North Korea, Iran), we offer secure cohorts with heightened privacy measures. No recordings are posted. Sessions are audio-only. Pseudonyms are welcome.

  • Audio-only meetings (no video)
  • Pseudonyms encouraged; no real names required
  • No posted recordings
  • Short written summaries in Dari or Pashto (when bilingual leader available)

Language & Access

Instruction is in English. For learners in restricted contexts, we offer secure cohorts with pseudonyms, audio-only meetings, and no posted recordings. Where a bilingual facilitator is available, we also provide short written summaries in Dari or Pashto.

Waypoints of Trust: Security Measures

We take operational security seriously. Here's how we protect learners in restricted contexts:

Minimal PII

We collect only what's necessary. Real names are never required for secure cohorts. Email addresses are stored in a separate identity store.

Separate Identity Store

Pseudonyms and course participation data are kept separate from identifiable information. Table leaders see only pseudonyms.

Data Purges

Secure cohort data (discussion logs, assignments) are purged on a fixed schedule after course completion.

Incident Response

We have protocols for responding to compromised accounts, legal inquiries, and threats to student safety. Table leaders are trained in basic opsec.

Signal/Wire for Secure Cohorts

Secure cohorts use end-to-end encrypted messaging (Signal or Wire) for coordination and file sharing.

No Posted Recordings

Secure cohorts never have recordings posted publicly or privately. All instruction is live and ephemeral.

For more information on our security stance, contact us via secure channels.
