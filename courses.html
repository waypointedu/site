Courses in Biblical Foundations & Spiritual Formation

Follow the guided one-year pathway or move at your own pace through orientation, Scripture, doctrine, culture, and formation courses with apologetics woven throughout.

Intro Seminar

Waypoint Orientation Seminar

2 weeks

An introduction to the learning system, pacing, and resources for success. This seminar equips learners with orientation in the LMS and the Waypoint community.

Term 1 — Foundations in Scripture & Learning

Learning how to read the Bible and understanding how to communicate God’s Word.

Hermeneutics

16 weeks

Principles and pitfalls for interpreting Scripture as taught by Jesus, used by the apostles, and practiced by the church across centuries. Includes apologetics on trustworthiness, contradictions, and canon.

Outcomes:

  • Understand, use, and teach methods to read, interpret, and apply the Word of God.
  • Answer objections about the reliability of the Bible, translations, and canon.

Old Testament: Torah, Prophets, Writings

16 weeks

Three five-week sections covering Torah, Prophets, and Writings. Focus on theology, context, and Christ-centered interpretation. Includes apologetics on Old Testament violence, laws, and suffering.

Outcomes:

  • Summarize Old Testament structure and themes: creation, covenant, worship, wisdom, faithfulness.

New Testament: Gospels & Acts

16 weeks

Focus on the Gospels and Acts as the climax of the biblical storyline. Includes apologetics on Jesus’ divinity, resurrection, and teachings.

Outcomes:

  • Identify the kingdom message of Jesus and the Spirit-empowered mission in Acts.
  • Explain why the Gospels are historically compelling.

Term 2 — The Apostolic Witness & the Unity of Scripture

Develop core doctrine and witness how Scripture interprets Scripture in Christ.

New Testament: Epistles & Revelation

16 weeks

Study the theology of the epistles and Revelation’s vision of Christ and worship. Apologetics on Paul’s message, cultural critiques, and Revelation as history and prophecy.

Outcomes:

  • Outline doctrines and pastoral directions of the Epistles.
  • Articulate Revelation’s vision of worship and hope.

New Testament Use of the Old Testament

16 weeks

Explore Old Testament prophecy, types, and fulfillment in Christ. Includes apologetics on biblical self-reference and coherence.

Outcomes:

  • Trace Old Testament to New Testament connections: law, temple, priesthood, sacrifice, Messiah.
  • Show how Christ fulfills the Law and Prophets.

Term 3 — Formation & Mission

Learning to live the gospel in culture through wise engagement and holiness.

Biblical Principles of Culture

16 weeks

The Bible on Christ as King and cultural reconciliation with focus on Romans 14–15. Apologetics on colonialism, pluralism, and relativism.

Outcomes:

  • Weigh cultural narratives with biblical categories.
  • Use wisdom in questions about idolatry and conscience.

Biblical Spiritual Practices

16 weeks

Biblical grounding for practices like prayer, fasting, and repentance. Apologetics on spirituality, divine knowledge, and ritual.

Outcomes:

  • Live a Rule of Life of prayer, Scripture, fellowship, and service.
  • Offer credible reasons for Christian practices in pluralistic contexts.
