Course Catalog (Pilot Semester)

Five foundational courses across Scripture, doctrine, culture, apologetics, and moral life. Each runs 5–10 weeks with weekly live or recorded sessions.

Biblical Theology I: Scripture, Canon, and Interpretation

Open

8 weeks · 90 min/week · CS-1

Outcomes:

  • Trace the narrative arc of Scripture from creation to new creation
  • Understand basic hermeneutical principles and historical-grammatical method
  • Practice liturgical and lectionary reading approaches
  • Engage the concept of canon and its formation

Christian Doctrine I: Creed, Trinity, Christology

Open

7 weeks · 90 min/week · CS-1

Outcomes:

  • Articulate the Nicene Creed and its theological implications
  • Explore classical Trinitarian theology
  • Understand Chalcedonian Christology and its challenges
  • Practice doctrinal reasoning in contemporary contexts

Theology of Culture & Media

Open

6 weeks · 90 min/week · CM-1

Outcomes:

  • Develop frameworks for reading culture theologically
  • Analyze representation of religion in film, television, and digital media
  • Exercise discernment in entertainment and social media consumption
  • Engage theories of meaning, symbol, and cultural narrative

Apologetics for Restricted Contexts

Secure

10 weeks · 90 min/week · MA-1

Outcomes:

  • Distinguish persuasion from coercion in witness
  • Navigate Christian witness under legal or social pressure
  • Respond to pastoral crises with theological and practical wisdom
  • Practice prudence in contexts where safety is at risk

Christian Moral Life: Virtue, Freedom, Community

Open

8 weeks · 90 min/week · CS-1

Outcomes:

  • Engage virtue ethics in the Christian tradition
  • Explore conscience, freedom, and moral formation
  • Apply moral reasoning to family and civic obligations
  • Analyze case studies in bioethics, justice, and discipleship

Note: Secure cohorts meet audio-only with pseudonyms; no recordings posted. For more information, see How it Works.
